# McBride Creative - Business Context Document

## Business Overview

**Business Name:** McBride Creative  
**Owner:** Jason McBride  
**Location:** Sault Ste. Marie, Ontario, Canada  
**Industry:** Marketing Design & Social Media for Community Theatre

### Mission
Professional marketing design for community theatre companies. Helping theatres fill seats with designs that deserve an encore.

### Core Philosophy
Theatre is about irreplaceable, live human connection and immersive experiences that cannot be replicated through screens (TV, devices). McBride Creative sells the feeling, connection, and immersion of live performance - marketing the experience of being in the room where it happens.

## Founder Background

### Professional Experience
- **Current:** Software Developer working with PHP, JavaScript, and web technologies
- **Companies:** CBN Lottery Group (tournament management systems, PIX payment processing, thin client applications)
- **Technical Skills:** PHP, JavaScript, Laravel, Rails, Angular, Redis, JWT, session management, web applications

### Creative Journey
- **Past 2 Years:** Developed expertise in Canva design and social media content creation
- **Theatre Involvement:** Active with Musical Comedy Guild (MCG) Sault Ste. Marie
  - Designed marketing materials for productions including Disney's The Little Mermaid and Little Shop of Horrors
  - Created social media content throughout production cycles (crew announcements, cast reveals, opening night, post-show wrap-up)
- **Design Evolution:** Transitioned from full-stack developer to specialized Canva designer for theatre

### Why This Business
After two years designing promotional materials for MCG and seeing firsthand how professional design impacts ticket sales and audience engagement, Jason is launching McBride Creative to serve community theatres across Canada.

## Target Market

### Primary Audience
Community theatre companies, specifically:
- Organizations with 4-8 productions per year
- Volunteer-run organizations with limited resources
- Groups that care about marketing (active on social media) but have inconsistent or amateur-looking graphics
- Theatre companies that understand the value of professional design but lack in-house expertise

### Geographic Focus
- **Home Base:** Sault Ste. Marie, Ontario
- **Service Area:** Theatres across Canada (online business model)
- **Initial Prospects:** Ontario and nearby regions

### Market Understanding
- Community theatres have tight budgets but pay for what they value
- They need help with marketing because they're typically volunteer-run
- Insider knowledge of theatre culture, season planning, subscription drives, audition promotion, and donor engagement is invaluable
- Decision-makers are often volunteer board members looking for trustworthy, professional help

## Brand Identity

### Brand Name Evolution
Extensive exploration of names including:
- Marquee Creative (taken)
- Playbill Creative (trademark concerns with established Playbill company)
- Fourth Wall Creative (exists in London with low activity)
- Various theatre-themed options (Spotlight House, Ovation Design, Curtain Call Co., etc.)

## Market Insights

### What Community Theatres Need
- Consistent visual identity across season
- Professional-looking materials that compete with other entertainment options
- Fast turnaround for show-specific materials
- Budget-conscious pricing with clear value
- Someone who understands their unique challenges and timelines

### What Makes Audiences Buy Tickets
- Professional, eye-catching design that stands out
- Clear show information and compelling visuals
- Consistent brand presence that builds trust
- Marketing that captures the excitement and connection of live theatre
- Messaging that emphasizes the unique, irreplaceable experience

### Pricing Considerations
- Community theatres have limited budgets
- They currently pay local print shops for basic design
- Price competitively while offering significantly more value
- Tiered packages allow entry points
- Season packages provide discount and commitment
- Consider intensive campaign periods (show openings, fundraising drives)
